Reading

Title: Identifies the topic and grabs readers' interest.


Introduction: Introduces the topic and sets up the kind of information that will follow in the body.


Body: Includes the main information of the article.


Conclusion: Sums up the entire article and restates the central idea.


Central Idea: The most important point a writer is trying to make about a topic.


Topic: The general subject of a passage


Supporting Idea: An idea that helps develop and tell more about a Central Idea


Supporting Details: Any kind of factual information that helps explain a supporting idea

Math

If an integer has a negative number and a positive number. If the positive number is larger than the negative number, the result will be positive. Same thing backwards.


If an integer has 2 numbers that are the same, the answer will be that number multiplied by 2


If a multiplication problem has one positive and one negative integer, the answer is always negative.


If a multiplication problem has two positive or two negative integers, the answer is always positive.
